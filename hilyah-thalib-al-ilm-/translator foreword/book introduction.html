Description of the Book

The Book: Hilyah Ṭālib al-ʿIlm - Ettiquettes of a student on seeking knowledge

Author: Bakr bin Abdullah Abu Zaid (d. 1429 AH)

Translated by: Abu ’Abdillah Murad ibn Hilmi Ash-Shuweik

Publisher: Dar al-Asima, Riyadh

Number of pages: 97

Translator's Foreword

Author's Foreword

Chapter 1: The Etiquette of the Seeker's Inner Self

  • 1. Knowledge is worship
  • 2. Be adherent to the path of the Salaf as-Saalih
  • 3. Commit to fearing Allaah, the Most High
  • 4. Continual observance [of Allaah]
  • 5. Lower your wing and put arrogance and pride behind you
  • 6. Contentment and zuhd (Asceticism)
  • 7. Adorn yourself with the splendour of knowledge
  • 8. Adorn [yourself] with honour.
  • 9. Enjoy masculine qualities.
  • 10. Forsaking luxury.
  • 11. To avoid the gatherings of vain speech.
  • 12. Avoid commotion (hayshaat)
  • 13. Adorn [yourself] with gentleness.
  • 14. Contemplation
  • 15. Firmness and confirmation

Chapter 2: The Methodology of Seeking Knowledge

  • 16. The methodology of seeking knowledge and its levels.
  • 17. To acquire knowledge from the mashaa'yikh (scholars)
