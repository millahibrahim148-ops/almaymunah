A Student’s Guide to Note-Taking & Review

Question

A question was asked to the brother regarding how can one have an effective note taking and review as a student of knowledge. The questioner mentioned having problems with excessive highlighting—turning entire pages into color due to marking every interesting point.

As the questioner is on the path of learning the Deen, the brother here clarifies how to properly have effective note-taking and review. May Allah increase us all in beneficial knowledge and make this a benefit for us all.

Answer

When you first approach a book—especially a topic new to you—everything feels new and amazing. Every page seems full of discoveries. This is normal and a beautiful experience. If you tried to take notes on every mind-blowing point, you would end up rewriting the entire book.

On Taking Notes

Once you become familiar with the material, fewer things will stand out as “new and special.” Then you can take meaningful notes. Understanding comes in layers: the deeper your general understanding, the fewer highlights you need.

On Memorization

Scholars say: *the one who truly has knowledge is the one who acts upon it.* Acting upon knowledge strengthens retention.

There are two levels of memorization:

  • 1 — Word-for-word memorization: Only suitable for Arabic texts; difficult and time-consuming.
  • 2 — Memorizing the meaning: Understanding and being able to summarize the idea in your own words.

On Summarizing & Re-Reading

One of the best ways to absorb a text is to summarize it in your own words. This requires rereading the book multiple times. Each pass deepens your understanding.

On Reviewing & Retention

True memorization comes from revisiting knowledge repeatedly. Many students memorize but fail to review, causing them to forget large portions. Review is what keeps knowledge alive.

Revisiting the same material several times—even simple books—cements understanding and prevents forgetfulness.

Final Advice

Knowledge needs renewal. If you don’t return to it, it fades—just like a subscription that expires unless renewed. As you grow in your studies, old highlights may seem unnecessary because your understanding has matured. Therefore, highlight conservatively and revisit often.
