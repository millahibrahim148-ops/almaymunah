Description of the Book

The Book: Hilyah Ṭālib al-ʿIlm - Etiquettes of a Student on Seeking Knowledge

Author: Bakr bin Abdullah Abu Zaid (d. 1429 AH)

Translated by: Abu ’Abdillah Murad ibn Hilmi Ash-Shuweik

Publisher: Dar al-Asima, Riyadh

Number of pages: 97

Contents

Translator's Foreword
Author's Foreword
Chapter 1: The Etiquette of the Seeker's Inner Self
1. Knowledge is worship 2. Be adherent to the path of the Salaf as-Saalih 3. Commit to fearing Allaah, the Most High 4. Continual observance [of Allaah] 5. Lower your wing and put arrogance and pride behind you 6. Contentment and zuhd (Asceticism) 7. Adorn yourself with the splendour of knowledge 8. Adorn [yourself] with honour. 9. Enjoy masculine qualities. 10. Forsaking luxury. 11. To avoid the gatherings of vain speech. 12. Avoid commotion (hayshaat) 13. Adorn [yourself] with gentleness. 14. Contemplation 15. Firmness and confirmation
Chapter 2: The Methodology of Seeking Knowledge...
16. The methodology of seeking knowledge and its levels. 17. To acquire knowledge from the mashaa'yikh (scholars)
Chapter 3: The Etiquette of the Seeker with his Shaykh....
18. Considering the shaykh's [consequent] status 19. Your capital - O seeker - is from your shaykh 20. The shaykh's enthusiasm in his lessons 21. Writing what the shaykh says during the lesson or revision. 22. Acquiring knowledge from a mubtadi (an innovator)
Chapter 4: The Etiquette of Companionship.
23. Beware of the bad companion
Chapter 5: The Etiquette of the Student in Leading a Life of Knowledge.
24. High aspirations in knowledge 25. The burning desire for seeking knowledge. 26. Emigrating for the sake of seeking knowledge 27. Preserving knowledge through writing [it] 28. Preservation (of knowledge) through paying attention to it... 29. Occasional revision of knowledge. 30. To gain understanding (of Islaam) by deriving the issues of figh (Islaamic rulings) by applying the [comprehensive] principles (of the Sharee'ah) 31. Seeking refuge in Allaah when seeking knowledge and acquiring it 32. Honesty in knowledge 33. Truthfulness 34. The sanctuary of the seeker of knowledge 35. Preserving your capital [i.e. lifetime] 36. Relaxing the body. 37. Precision and correction reading 38. Summarising long compilations [through reading] 39. Excellence in asking questions 40. Debating without argumentation 41. Revision of the knowledge in groups 42. The seeker of knowledge lives between the [texts of the] Qur'aan and Sunnah and knowledge associated to them. 43. Completion of learning the tools of every subject.
Chapter 6: Adorn Yourself with the Implementation of Knowledge
44. The signs of beneficial knowledge 45. The alms of knowledge. 46. The glory of the scholars 47. Protection of knowledge. 48. Diplomacy not compromise (on behalf of the religion) 49. Passion for the books. 50. The foundation of your library 51. Dealing with the book 52. Further to this. 53. Writing with diacritical points
Chapter 7: Precautions.
54. False hopes 55. Beware of being "Aboo Shibr" (the father of a span) 56. Becoming manifest before becoming qualified. 57. Deterioration of manners in knowledge 58. (Merely) "putting pen to paper" 59. Your stance on the mistakes of those who preceded you....... 60. Deterring dubious affairs. 61. Beware of grammatical errors (in Arabic). 62. Beware of premature intellectuality 63. Beware of the new Israa'eeliyaat 64. Beware of Byzantine debate 65. No walaa' and baraa' (declaring allegiance and declaring innocence) should be pledged over sectarianism and partisanship. 66. The things that nullify this hilyah.
